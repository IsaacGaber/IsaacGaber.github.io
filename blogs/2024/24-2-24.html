Control-J User Manual

Stumped. I sit with no idea as to which words logically follow the ones I allegedly marked down onto the page. Nothing left, nothing to look forward to. A flash of light and mechanical inspiration is here. Algorithms burn and convolute only to spawn meaningless digits. The word thief rides again. Meaningless mechanical monotony masquerades as magic.

Sometimes I can't finish a sentence. What's the right verb, adjective, or noun? I don't know! Overwhelmed and stranded on the precipice, I wrote Control-J. The Program. I slip over the edge.

The Program is a mechanical plagiarism assistant disguised as a text editor. You may write sentences tailed by punctuation; like in a text editor. Or, you could leave an idea half-finished and trailing off. Just when the sentence begins to de-adhere, you may press "CTRL-J" and let the program finish the rest.

"Control-J" steals words and phrases from Jane Austen's Brain (Pride and Prejudice) and completes (or hopes to complete) the last sentence you have entered. This program is not original, clever, or coherent.
