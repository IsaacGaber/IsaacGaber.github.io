A. Study Plan

 

  1. 1.What is the purpose of your application to Yonsei University? (at least 10 sentences) 

 

Yonsei is one of the world’s foremost international universities. Having studied Mandarin Chinese and Japanese language before in the past, I now wish to study Korean language. Thus, during my time at Yonsei University I hope to begin learning Korean language, further my study of design and if I am able, continue my study of Chinese language as well. College-level Korean language programs are few and far between in the states, which has pushed me towards Yonsei. I have heard from former Yonsei students about the excellent classes, facilities, and extracurricular activities available. Therefore I know that while at Yonsei I will benefit from both world-class educational opportunities and simultaneous immersion in Korean language and culture.  Given this, I believe the college offers the best experience for me to study abroad in Korea.  Never before have I been able to fully immerse myself fully in a foreign language and culture the way studying at Yonsei will enable me to do. Unlike my home institution, Yonsei has a much larger campus, vastly larger student population, far more international reach, and a wider variety of courses to take. Yonsei will equip me with the tools I need for my international future.

 

  1. 2.What is the major you want to study at Yonsei University and why? (at least 10 sentences) 

 

At Yonsei, I hope to study in the The College of Human Ecology's Department of Integrated Design. The Major’s focus on design is something which I find hard to replicate within my Sarah Lawrence classwork. While my college has many options for humanities courses, it offers few classes which deal directly with the elements of design in which I am most interested. Namely, interactive media and architecture. In The College of Human Ecology’s Department of Integrated Design I hope to accomplish this. Honing my senses for visual and interaction design will be essential if I hope to make a career out of video game design. Doing so in an intensive academic environment will do measures to cement my skills. Currently, I want to focus on my drawing, my sense of scale, and my sense of space as components of my design repertoire that have the most room for improvement. All of these components are essential to creating video games. Thus, all of them are essential to my future.

B. Self Description

 

  1. 1.Self-introduction: Please explain and describe your aptitudes, hobbies, and philosophy of life (at least 10 sentences) 

 

I truly believe interdisciplinary education is essential to fully understanding and working within the world. As such, while my primary interests are in the video game design industry and interactive media I also have other, tangentially related interests. Specifically: Languages, Geography, History, and Art. All of these interests I have been able to study in equal measure at Sarah Lawrence College, and I have discovered that I am particularly fascinated by Geography and History. In my spare time, I love walking for miles through City and through Nature, and especially where the two extremes intersect. Additionally, I regularly bike long distances and am looking forward to exploring South Korea’s mountains and valleys on two wheels.

 

Post-graduation, I aim to eventually turn my video game designing into a career. I don’t expect it to be easy, but I do believe that South Korea is one of the places to be for aspiring game developers due to the country’s burgeoning Indie and AAA game development scenes. Recent international hits like Nexon’s Dave the Diver have proved that Korea’s game designers have global reach. Meanwhile, events like the annual Busan Indie Connect further share these developments with the world. During my time in Korea, I see myself taking part with and engaging with these tides of change.  Nothing else would make me happier.

 

  1. 2.Educational Background: Please summarize your academic achievements (at least 10 sentences) 

 

During my time at Sarah Lawrence college, I have engaged deeply with history and politics through my academic study of East Asia. In particular, the contradictions brought about by “development,” specifically in China. I have used my Chinese language skills to gain a deeper understanding of these contradictions, and to further communicate those understandings in Chinese and English, through both writing and art. Essential to my experience at Sarah Lawrence College so far has been taking advantage of the opportunities provided to incorporate all my various interests into an interdisciplinary curriculum. For example, I spent last semester working on a research project and paper in a Chinese Geography class where I mapped the interactions between Chinese science fiction and the urban realities of Contemporary China. I looked particularly at the fiction of Han Song and the reality of the short-lived Zhejiang Village slum near Beijing's Dahongmen. Through the comparison, I hoped to answer fundamental questions about China, such as who does China's 'development' serve? And, how does an especially imaginative and particularly Chinese science fiction further understanding of this distinct form of development?

 

While my studies have focused on China thus far, I want to expand my horizons by focusing now on Korea. Although Korea has intimate historical, cultural, and culinary ties to Chinese societies, the country is wildly distinct and therefore a logical next site to foster my academic inquiry. I hope to adapt my academic techniques to learn more about Korea and Korean culture.

 

In addition, this semester I am working on a project I plan to exhibit for a literature class. The project itself is a text editor which I programmed to auto-complete the user’s sentences using a custom algorithm. The algorithm finds quotes it deems appropriate from Jane Austen’s Pride and Prejudice and finishes the sentence with them. When the exhibit is installed, visitors will be able to print out and take home whatever they write using the program using a modified receipt printer running custom software.

 

I know that exposing myself firsthand to the world's art and culture is necessary to my own artistic development. Further, it is essential to further understanding of universal and particular aspects of society and culture in my academic work.
